Lane Detection Dataset Collecetion

Published: Sun 03 October 2021
Updated: Thu 14 October 2021
By Shijie Wu

In Computer Vision.

In a vision based autonomous driving pipeline, static and moving objects are detected with computer vision methods. Historically, lanes are detected with a sliding window. Currently, the methods become computer vision models. Among static object detection there are lane detection. And here is a list of public lane detection datasets.

Published in 2017

  • Mapillary Vistas dataset has a total of 25k images with data collected globally. Below is the sample ground truth, the white part is the lane.

  • TuSimple has a total of 72k images. They are collected from highway with clearr vision of lane textures. All lanes are annotated with polylines.

  • CULane contains 98k images with categories of like crowded, night, no line, shadow. This dataset was collected at Beijing in 6 taxis.

Published in 2018

  • BDD100k contains 120 million images. It also contains lane mark annotations.

  • ApolloScape contains 144k images.

Published in 2019

  • Unsupervised LLAMAS dataset is distinctive in that it uses HD map to localize ego car and project maps onto ego frames.

Published in 2020

  • CurveLanes contains 150k images collected across China, the lanes were biased to have big curvatures.

Conclusion

TuSimple and CULane are the most commonly used datasets in publications in lane detection. Among both CULane are more challenging because the diversity in scenarios.
